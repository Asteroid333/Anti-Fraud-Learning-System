Scam Characteristics

This course introduces common suspicious characteristics of scams. The aim is to help users learn to distinguish suspicious messages.

1. Creating Urgency

Scam messages often create urgency to force immediate action without thinking.

"Your account will be frozen in 24 hours, please click the link immediately to verify your identity."

"Limited time offer, only 2 spots left!"

2. Requesting Sensitive Information

Legitimate institutions typically don't request passwords, verification codes, or other sensitive information via email or text.

"Please reply to this text with your login password to complete verification."

"Please enter the verification code you received to confirm your identity."

3. Grammar and Spelling Errors

Many scam messages contain obvious grammar errors, spelling mistakes, or unnatural expressions.

"Dear customer, your account has abnormal activity, please quickly click link to handle."

"Congratulations you won! Please provide your bank information for us to send prize."

4. Suspicious Sender Addresses

Check if the sender's email or phone number matches official information.

Official email: service@bank-official.com

Scam email: service@bank-security.com

Note subtle differences

Identify Web Links

This course provides examples of real and fake links, teaching you how to identify correct webpage links.

Real Links vs Fake Links

Real Link Characteristics

  • Use correct domain names (e.g., bank-official.com)
  • URL starts with https:// and has a lock icon
  • Correct spelling, no extra characters
  • Matches official promoted links
  • Links are concise, without long random strings
Real Link Examples:
https://www.bank-official.com/login
https://www.paypal.com/account/verify

Fake Link Characteristics

  • Misspelled domains (e.g., bank-offical.com)
  • Use similar character substitutions (e.g., 0 instead of o, 1 instead of l)
  • Contain extra subdomains (e.g., bank.security-login.com)
  • URLs are too long with many random characters
  • Use http:// instead of https://
Fake Link Examples:
http://bank-offical.com.login.secure-verify.net
https://paypa1.com.secure-account.xyz/login

How to Safely Check Links:

1

Hover to View

Hover over the link to see the actual URL displayed in the bottom left of the browser

2

Check Domain

Confirm the main domain is correct, watch for spelling errors and character substitutions

3

Use Secure Connections

Only visit websites starting with https:// to ensure encrypted connections

4

Verify Through Official Channels

Access directly through official apps or websites, not by clicking links

Risk Assessment

This course explains how to assess the risk of being scammed. It uses a simple flowchart to illustrate the steps and color-codes different risk levels. Users can learn how to evaluate the danger level in real-world situations.

Step 1: Receive Suspicious Message

Check if the information source is reliable, has grammatical errors, or urgent demands.

Step 2: Check Links or Attachments

If it's a link, check if the URL is legitimate; if it's an attachment, confirm if it was expected.

Step 3: Is Personal Information Requested?

Is there a request for passwords, verification codes, bank card information, or other sensitive data?

Step 4: Does It Involve Money?

Is there a request for transfers, payment of fees, or provision of financial information?

Risk Level Explanation

Low Risk

Information from reliable sources, no suspicious features, doesn't involve personal information or money.

Medium Risk

Some information is suspicious but doesn't yet involve sensitive data. Needs further verification.

High Risk

Clearly requests personal information or money, has multiple scam characteristics. Should stop interaction immediately.

Simulation Feedback

Analysis Explanation

Suspicious Feature Analysis:

  • Suspicious Link: Uses http instead of https, domain bank-security-verify.com is not the bank's official domain
  • Creates Urgency: Requires completion within 30 minutes, otherwise account freeze
  • Requests Sensitive Information: Requests bank card number, ID number, and verification code
  • Non-Official Channel: Banks typically don't request such comprehensive sensitive information via text

Safe Response Analysis:

The safest approach is not to click any links, directly contact the bank through official channels (like the customer service number found on the bank's official website) to verify the situation. This avoids falling into phishing websites while confirming the account's real status.

Learning Points:

When encountering such messages, never verify through links or phone numbers in the message. Use official contact methods you find yourself to verify.
